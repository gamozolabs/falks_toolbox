Formulas (Machinery's Handbook 29th Ed, p.2030):
Unified: Hole = Major − (1.08253 × %Thread / TPI)
Metric: Hole = Major − (1.08253 × Pitch × %Thread)

Drill Matching: Letter (A-Z) and Number (#1-#80) drills shown when within ±0.08 mm (±3 thou). Fractional drills (1/64") always shown — see color legend below. Hover over any drill size for exact dimensions and deviations.

Sources & References:
  • Machinery's Handbook 29th Ed, p.2020: "Tests have shown that any increase in the percentage of full thread over 60 per cent does not significantly increase the strength of the thread."
  • Machinery's Handbook 29th Ed, p.2020: "Often, a 55 to 60 per cent thread is satisfactory, although 75 per cent threads are commonly used to provide an extra measure of safety."
  • Machinery's Handbook 29th Ed, p.2020: "The tap drill should not be smaller than is necessary to give the required strength to the thread as even a very small decrease in the diameter of the drill will increase the torque required and the possibility of broken taps."
  • Machinery's Handbook 29th Ed, p.2018 (Stainless): "Austenitic stainless steels are very difficult to tap because of their high resistance to cutting and their great tendency to work harden."
